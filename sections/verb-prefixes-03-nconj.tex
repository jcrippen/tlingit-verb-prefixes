\section{\textit{N}-conjugation prefix \textit{n-}}\label{sec:nconj}

\begin{multicols}{2}
\noindent
The \fm{n-} conjugation prefix is the marker of membership in the \fm{n}-conjugation class.
As such, forms with \fm{n-} occur for any \fm{n}-conjugation verb when a conjugation prefix is selected.
The \fm{n-} conjugation prefix also has a dedicated use in progressive aspect forms, similar to how the \fm{g-} conjugation prefix is used for prospective aspect.
\end{multicols}
